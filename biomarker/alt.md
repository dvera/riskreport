## Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT)


Alanine Transaminase (ALT) is an enzyme found mostly in the liver. An ALT test measures the amount of ALT in the blood. When liver cells are damaged, they release ALT into the bloodstream. High levels of ALT in your blood may be a sign of a liver injury or other adverse outcomes. Some types of adverse liver outcomes cause high ALT levels before you have symptoms. Thus, ALT blood tests may help diagnose certain liver outcomes early.
