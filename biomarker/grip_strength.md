## Hand Grip Strength


Grip Strength is a simple measure of how much force you generate when you squeeze with your hand. It is tested by squeezing a device called a dynamometer, which records the strength of your grip. This measurement is not just about hand strength; it is also a quick indicator of overall muscle strength and health. Studies show lower grip strength often is linked to a higher prevalence of certain health issues, like heart or mobility problems, especially in older adults. Tracking grip strength helps clinicians assess muscle health, recovery progress, or prevalence of future age-related illness.
