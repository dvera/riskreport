## IGF1


IGF-1 (insulin-like growth factor 1) is a hormone, a chemical messenger in the bloodstream controlling the actions of certain cells or organs. IGF-1 manages the effects of growth hormone (GH) in the body. Together, IGF-1 and GH promote normal growth of bones and tissues. GH levels in the blood change throughout the day, depending on diet and activity levels, while IGF-1 levels are more stable, therefore a reliable way to track GH is by measuring the level of IGF-1 in the blood. Levels of IGF-1 and GH change throughout an individual's lifetime. Besides growth, GH also helps control metabolism, which is the process of generating energy from food. Some conditions, such as pituitary tumors, can cause the body to produce too much or too little GH.
